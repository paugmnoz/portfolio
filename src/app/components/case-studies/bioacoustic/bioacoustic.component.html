Studying nature bioacoustics.


An improved scientific software proposal to analyze bioacoustics and counting bird species.


Role

Timeframe

Jan 2019 - Dec 2019

Colombia, like one of the most biodiverse countries in the world, needs a way to quantify its biodiversity and facilitate its protection. A start to accomplish that objective is to implement remote bioacoustic monitoring. Colombia's natural zones should be recorded to keep counts of the biodiversity.

Who is involved ?


Bioacoustic monitoring are usually made by groups of biology professionals. Basically every professional studying nature from the sounds of species. However, this project focused on an ornithology context, since that discipline is the one that uses bioacoustics the most.

The process of a bioacoustic monitoring study:

How does bioacoustic monitoring work?


In our context approach, we made several interviews with ornithologists and herpetologist to get to know this labor. From all of them we conclude this is the general experience of doing a bioacoustics study:




1. Record Data.

One of the most recording protocols used is to record by counting points. Which consists taking the recording as a complement for a visual-field study (counting birds by eye). Biologist record every station meanwhile they are looking for species in the area, when they come back to the laboratory they listen the records to verify what they found and to find any other species not listed in field.


The other protocol is using an ARU (Automatic Record Unit) is to left it with a programmed schedule for a period of time. In that case, biologists don't have an idea of what they will found more than technical information like species of the area. But due to the absence of humans around, a more natural behavior study can have more interesting results.



2. Data gathering from recorders.

Data is usually manually collected in SD memory cards once or multiple times. Sometimes is possible to collect it digitally, when the area has good network signals what is usually not.



3. Data Review.

Once data was gathered, biologist can review the audios in their computers. The need to play them in software that let them see the spectrogram of the sound and adjust sound characteristics to help them identify any bird sounds.



4. Data label and storage.

Once a sound is identified, biologists make a list in which they put the species and study information with the audio trimmed.


Doing bioacoustic studies requires a large use of digital software to review data and to label it proprerly. How great is the experience of using a labelling software and carrying out a bioacoustic study?




This is a task that can still be improved, especially, when we see it from a digital product experience view.



First, let's see what’s happening


Besides, there is already a protocol and a usual process to do bioacoustic monitoring, this is a task that can still be improved. Especially, when we see it from a digital product experience view. In our early steps in this project, my partner, Sebastián, and I conducted the following methods to get acquainted with the bioacoustic monitoring experience.

Multiple expert interviews with professionals.


Observation of the main task: Data Review, Label, and Storage with professionals.


Immersion with 5 software.


From these qualitative methods, we made an affinity diagram that gave us the scope of the project. In the first place, it let us know the professional's perception for realizing this task and, on the other hand, the context and understanding of the experience design performance of the most popular software used. With those, we could identify the main issues that are hindering the bioacoustic monitoring task, then start wondering and asking ourselves what can we do, as designers, to minimize those pains.

There are lots of records without been analyzed and without a storage order.


80% of the professionals agreed with the fact of having lots of recordings of important zones without been analyzed and distributed in multiple external hard drives. They all said the reason was a lack of people and time to listen and label the species recorded in all those registers. What can we do to help them to organize and manage these files? And is there a way to reduce the time of review and labeling for those?

In Colombia, there are not enough qualified people who can carry out this type of study.


Despite being the 2nd country most biodiverse, the percentage of potential professionals that could perform these studies is relatively low. As we can see in the chart below. Is there something we can do to encourage and become the bioacoustic monitoring a more engaging experience?

The first experience with the majority of software results overwhelming.


From all software we download and use as newbies into this task, our first impression can be resumed in a quote “Where should I start? and Do I need to use all of these buttons?”.


Almost every possible action in the software was displayed since the beginning. And this often passes with scientific software, like ****, it seems like user interface design is not a primordial aspect when developing them. As expected, scientific software are not easy to learn to use them, because they are created to do specific and complex (several) actions that a computers cand make faster; somehow, these should help the people to do those complex actions in a simple and unconfusing experience, and this can be achieved with good information architecture.

Limited actions on the main tool for the study.


As we know, bioacoustic monitoring is mostly used with birds, and spectrograms are the key to label the species. However, the immersion showed us that the 75% of the software didn’t let the user interact that much with spectrograms, moreover, the professionals end up using multiple software that in the first place let them interact with the spectrogram in a more customized way, but also due to simplicity that gives them. Simplicity? Yeah. It is related to the overwhelming experience mentioned before. They look for simpler ways to do what the need.

The software interfaces always look the same, there’s no easy way to know what are you doing.


Taking the immersion with a design view, we found that the interfaces don’t give strong feedback about their state, everything is gray and it turns a bit exhausting. You can not take a break if you are not finished, otherwise, when you come back you will found you a bit lost in the software.



Now, answering the questions that every issue made us do ourselves, and according to the fact that most of the issues were related with the digital tools researchers use, we propose Bioviz.
