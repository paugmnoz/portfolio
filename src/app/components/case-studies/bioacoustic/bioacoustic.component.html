BIOVIZ

User experience - Research - Data Visualization

Studying nature bioacoustics.


Design for an artificial intelligence output and data visualization in a scientific software to analyze bioacoustics and counting bird species.


Role

UX Researcher



Timeframe

Jan 2019 - Present

Colombia, as the one of the most biodiverse countries in the world, needs a way to quantify its biodiversity. One of the best way to do it is to implement remote bioacoustic monitoring in their natural zones.


With this method, biologists get the data to find the different species living in the ecosystem and to analyze relationships between them.

!

However, this method
let big amount of acoustic data
that demands time to be
listened and analyzed.

Doing bioacoustic monitoring, with the actual tools, implies:


Species knowledge and expertise

The analyst has to have a background with the sounds a specie makes and their spectrogram representation


A lot of time

Labeling the species present in all audio files demands hours even months.


Order

Having all the audios organized and labeled can result complicated if there's multiple projects and the great quantity of data that each one has. If there's a team, it's difficult to know the progress if there's not an order.

We need to optimize the large acoustic database management and visualization so biologist or amateurs with few expertise can help with bioacoustic research.

I'm currently working in the solution proposed in this project.
