WOOF ME

User experience - Research

Taking care of your friend even when you’re not home.


A technology-enhanced training system for reducing separation anxiety in dogs.

Role

UX Researcher


Timeframe

2018 - 2019 (1 year, 1 month)

Woof me is a short research project I worked along with a college friend and a Professor from i2t (Intformatics and telecomunication research group).


Dogs are highly social animals, however when left alone, separation anxiety may appear with destructive behaviors. One of the most succesful treatments for canine separation-related problems involves behavior modification: systematic desensitization and counter-conditioning.

!

However, to implement
such treatment requires
a strong commitment
for the owner.

Systematic desensitization

Time consuming


Counter-conditioning

Right positive stimulation.

How to make easy a treatment to reduce separation anxiety, so dog owners can worry less when they’re not home?

!

Have in mind

Behaviors categorized by:

  • Anxious vocalizations
  • Physical uneasiness
  • Destructive behavior
  • Aggressiveness toward people

Dog sizes

Dog-owner relationship and times

WEARABLE VEST DESIGN

The vest was designed so the dog feel it like a hug, in addition it has components that let know the movement of the dog and to positive stimulate when anxiety was perceived by the system itslef.

Pilot Vest
System

Adjustable soft vest used
during the experiment.

The experimental design was implemented on 5 dogs:
Three beagles, one pug and one schnauzer.

MOBILE APPLICATION
DESIGN

The APP is the owners guide that will help them to train their dogs. With the training sessions the system will be calibrated with the dog characteristics and so the anxiety behaviors predicted will be accurate.
